How AI Works · Chapter 2

Wait, what is a token?

We've been throwing the word "tokens" around. Time to stop. Before a model can read any text, the text gets chopped into small pieces called tokens — and they're often smaller than whole words. Here's the sentence from Chapter 1, split into the tokens GPT-4 would actually see.

Tokenized by GPT-4
The fastest way to win in court is to
9 tokens 37 characters · ~4.1 chars/token

A few things worth noticing:

  • Every word here is one token, but that's a coincidence. This sentence is all common English. If we swapped in a rarer word like "jurisprudence" or "habeas," you'd see it split across multiple tokens.
  • Spaces attach to the following token, not the previous one. That's why " fastest" includes a leading space — there's no separate "space" token between "The" and "fastest."
  • Identical tokens share an identical ID. The two " to" tokens in this sentence are both token number 311 in GPT-4's vocabulary. A token is really just a number; the word is how we display it.
  • Every model family tokenizes differently. Claude, LLaMA, and GPT-4 each use their own vocabularies, so this exact sentence would break into different pieces depending on which model you asked.
